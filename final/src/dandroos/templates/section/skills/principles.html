My Development Principles

No matter what the scale or budget is, I follow these principles for all projects I undertake.

KISS

KISS stands for 'Keep It Simple, Stupid!'. The temptation with all these available languages/frameworks/tools is to over-complicate. To avoid this, I carefully plan my projects before writing any code. Everything is there for a purpose.

D.R.Y.

DRY stands for 'Don't Repeat Yourself'. Without careful planning, code can quickly become repetitious and therefore a nightmare to maintain! I ensure that any changes/updates to functionality need only to be made in one place.

S.O.C.

SOC stands for 'Separation of Concerns'. From a maintenance and scalability perspective, it is very important to separate concerns by modularising code. This allows making efficient changes to apps without breaking everything!

K.I.T.

KIT stands for 'Keep in Touch'. I pride myself on keeping my clients informed at regular pre-arranged intervals about the progress of their projects. That said, I set achievable deadlines from the start and do everything within my power to stick to them. I am 'on call' for my clients around the clock for a full 'post-sale' service and have a friendly, approachable manner.
